Opposition Kickout Strategy 3: The Zonal Press

Mark the spaces, not the men — shuffle across zones with hands up, deny short options, force a long contestable kick

Kickout Strategy 4: Over-the-Top Kicking

GK kicks deep over the retreating defence — fast half-forwards sprint behind the landing spot to win breaks

Attacking Strategy 5: Chalk on the Boots

Wing forwards and corner forwards hug the sidelines — forces defenders to track them, stretches the blanket, creates central gaps and 1v1 isolations

Our players Wide runners (chalk on boots) Opposition (stretched)

Opposition Kickout Strategy 6: Kickout Setup (v Ballinagh)

Press up on opposition kickouts with three lines of pressure:
Line 1: 13 & 15 push up to their 45 to cut off short options
Line 2: 12, 14, 11, 10 hold between midfield and their 45
Line 3: 5, 8, 6, 9, 7 sit at midfield — if the kick goes long left or right, 6 drops in behind to cover the channel. If it goes long down the middle, 5 & 7 drop in behind to cut it out.
