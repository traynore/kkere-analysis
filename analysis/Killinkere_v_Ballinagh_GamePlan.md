# Killinkere v Ballinagh — Tactical Game Plan
## Intermediate Championship Rd 1

---

| Vulnerability | Evidence | Exploitability |
|---|---|---|
| 1st-half kickout retention: **49%** | Lost 51% of their own restarts in opening halves across 3 games | 🔴 Critical |
| "Break Lost" on kickouts (28 total) | Win the initial contest but lose the break — weak secondary effort | 🔴 Critical |
| Shooting from distance (42% outside 45m) | 31% conversion from range; 8 shots "Short" in 3 games | 🟡 Moderate |
| Free conversion: **57%** | 6 missed frees across 3 games | 🟡 Moderate |

---

## How Killinkere Can Exploit These

### 1. 🎯 KICKOUT PRESSURE — THE #1 PRIORITY

**The data is clear:** Ballinagh's kickout is their Achilles heel, particularly in the 1st half. Every team that has pressured their restarts has built big leads.

#### What Killinkere should do:

**Set up aggressively on Ballinagh kickouts from the start:**
- Ballinagh's kickouts predominantly land in the middle third (Location Y 0.15–0.73 in the data). They rarely go short — only 1 short kickout across 3 games.
- Station **2 players in the middle sector** to contest the break. Ballinagh's issue isn't losing the initial aerial contest — it's losing the **secondary ball**. Killinkere need bodies around the break.
- Assign a **designated sweeper** behind the kickout contest to hoover up any ball that spills through.

**Killinkere's evidence they can do this:**
- v Ballymchugh, Killinkere won turnovers from opposition kickouts consistently — Jason McMahon, Kevin McCabe, Alex Sweeney, Mark Farrelly (J), and James Farrelly all won breaks or clean possession from Ballymchugh restarts.
- Killinkere forced **8 kickout turnovers** from Ballymchugh (5 Break Lost + 3 Lost Clean) in the 2nd half alone.

**Target:** Win possession from **at least 5 of Ballinagh's first 10 kickouts**. If achieved, this alone could yield 4–5 scoring opportunities in the opening 20 minutes.

---

### 2. ⚡ ATTACK IMMEDIATELY AFTER KICKOUT TURNOVERS

**The data shows:** When Ballinagh lose their kickout, they are disorganised defensively. Opponents scored within 60 seconds of a kickout turnover on **14 occasions** across 3 games.

#### What Killinkere should do:

- **No recycling** after winning a Ballinagh kickout — go direct. The defence is out of shape because bodies have pushed up for the restart.
- Identify **2 runners** who break forward the moment the kickout is contested. If the break is won, hit them immediately.
- v Cuchulainns, 6 of their 8 first-half points came directly from Ballinagh kickout turnovers. Killinkere should aim for the same.

**Killinkere's evidence they can do this:**
- v Ballymchugh 2nd half: Killinkere scored 5 times directly after winning Ballymchugh kickouts (events 93, 107, 114, 120, 131). The pattern is already there — win the restart, attack fast, score.

---

### 3. 🛡️ FORCE BALLINAGH TO SHOOT FROM DISTANCE

**The data shows:** Ballinagh convert only **31% from outside the 45m line** and 21% of all their shots go "Short" (don't reach the posts). Their danger zone is inside the 21m line (64% conversion).

#### What Killinkere should do:

- **Drop the defensive line to the 45m mark.** Concede territory in the middle third but pack the scoring zone.
- Force Ballinagh to shoot from range — they'll miss more than they score. Their free-taker (likely Kevin Smith) is reliable from close range but the team's overall accuracy drops sharply with distance.
- **Do NOT foul inside the 45m line** — Ballinagh's Kevin Smith converts 80% of close-range frees. Force them to earn scores from play at distance instead.

**Key defenders to assign:**
- Ballinagh's main threats from play: Kevin Smith, Ronan Connolly (scored 2 goals v Gowna), Player 6/Player 15 (v Cuchulainns)
- Connolly is the goal threat — he needs a dedicated marker who stays goalside at all times.

---

### 4. 🏃 EXPLOIT THE 2ND-HALF ADJUSTMENT WINDOW

**The data shows:** Ballinagh improve their kickout retention dramatically at half-time (43% → 100% v Cuchulainns). This means they make tactical adjustments at the break.

#### What Killinkere should do:

- **Build the lead in the 1st half** through kickout pressure (as above).
- **Expect Ballinagh to change at half-time** — likely going shorter or varying their kickout target. Have a plan B ready:
  - If they go short: press the short option with a designated rusher.
  - If they vary long: drop one of the middle-sector contesters back to sweep.
- **The first 5 minutes of the 2nd half are critical** — maintain intensity on the restart before Ballinagh's adjustment takes full effect.

---

### 5. 📊 FREE-TAKING — MAKE THEM PAY

**The data shows:** Ballinagh convert only 57% of scoreable frees. Eamon McEvoy misses 75% of his.

#### What Killinkere should do:

- **Win frees in the scoring zone.** Killinkere's free-takers (Emmett Fitzsimons 83%, Darren Smith 100% v Ballymchugh) are significantly more reliable than Ballinagh's.
- Every free won inside the 45m is essentially a guaranteed point for Killinkere vs a coin-flip for Ballinagh.
- Draw fouls by running at the Ballinagh defence — the data shows Ballinagh concede frees in their defensive third regularly (4–5 per game).

**Killinkere free-taking v Ballymchugh:**
| Taker | Taken | Scored | Conversion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emmett Fitzsimons | 5 | 4 | 80% |
| Darren Smith | 4 | 4 | 100% |
| **Total** | **9** | **8** | **89%** |

This is a massive advantage over Ballinagh's 57%.

## Game Plan Summary

| Phase | Killinkere Action | Expected Yield |
|---|---|---|
| **Ballinagh kickout (1st half)** | 2 in middle sector + sweeper behind. Contest the break aggressively. | Win 5+ of first 10 kickouts |
| **Transition after turnover** | Go direct — 2 runners break forward on every kickout contest | 4–5 scores from kickout turnovers |
| **Defensive shape** | Drop to 45m line, pack inside. Force shots from distance. | Reduce Ballinagh conversion to ~30% |
| **Attacking strategy** | Run at defence, draw frees inside 45m. Work ball to inside 21m for shots from play. | High free count + high conversion |
| **2nd half adjustment** | Expect Ballinagh to change kickout. Have press/sweep options ready. | Maintain pressure through transition |
